Instructional Design and Technology (IDT) IDT is a multidisciplinary field focused on creating effective, efficient, and engaging learning experiences using systematic design processes and technological tools. It combines principles of learning theories, instructional strategies, and emerging technologies to develop educational materials and systems for diverse audiences. IDT aims to enhance teaching and learning outcomes by leveraging research-based methodologies and innovations. Overview of IDTâ€™s History The evolution of IDT can be traced through several key phases: Pre-20th Century: Foundations in Pedagogy Early roots of instructional design can be seen in the work of educators like John Dewey, who emphasized experiential learning. The use of tools such as chalkboards and printed materials marked the first integration of "technology" in education. Early 20th Century: Emergence of Systematic Approaches Behaviorism (e.g., B.F. Skinner) laid the groundwork for instructional systems design, emphasizing observable learning outcomes and reinforcement. World War II spurred advancements in instructional design as the military developed systematic training programs to efficiently prepare large groups of personnel. 1950s-1970s: Rise of Instructional Systems Design (ISD) The ADDIE Model (Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation) emerged as a standard instructional design framework. Cognitive psychology influenced instructional design, shifting focus from observable behaviors to mental processes like memory and problem-solving. Technologies like television and computers began supporting educational delivery.
